Itinery

Day 1Airport- Amboseli National Park

Pick up from the Airport followed by transfer to Amboseli National Park. The park is famous for its big game – elephants, lions and cheetahs – and for its great charming beauty. Amboseli embodies five main wildlife habitats, plus a generally dry lake-bed – Lake Amboseli. These are open plains: extensive stands of yellow-barked acacia woodland, rocky, lava strewn thorn-bush country, swamps and marshes, and at the western end of the reserve, above Namanga, the massif of Ol Doinyo Orok rising to over 2,760m (8.300ft). The rising is still for the most part zoologically unexplored.


Day 2Amboseli National Park

Enjoy an early morning and afternoon game drives in Amboseli National Park. Maasai cultural visits can be arranged at a minimal extra cost payable direct. Full board accommodation is included in the tour price. Dinner and overnight: Kibo Safari Camp.


Day 3Amboseli-Lake Nakuru

Depart after your morning breakfast for Lake Nakuru. Upon arrival, you will rest for a while before embarking on an evening game drive within the 180 sq. km Lake Nakuru National. The park is home to warthogs, waterbuck, buffaloes, reedbucks, and the occasional leopard. White and black rhinos were introduced to the park some years ago for conservation purposes and you may find white rhinos at the southern end. The park has also retained its reputation as an ornithologists’ delight with more than 400 species of bird found here.


Day 4Lake Nakuru-Maasai Mara

Depart after your morning breakfast for the Maasai Mara Game Reserve. The next two days will be spent game viewing, either in the early morning hours or in the evening as this is when you will have the best chances of seeing animals in their natural setting. The Mara reserve measures approximately 1510 sq. km of incredible wide-open landscapes and fertile riverine woodland following the looping meanders of the Mara and Talek Rivers. Wherever you go in the Mara, you will see an abundant array of wildlife such as the Maasai Giraffe, baboons, warthogs, bat eared foxes, grey jackals, spotted hyena, topis, impala, hartebeests, wildebeest. Elephants, buffaloes, zebras and hippos also exist in great numbers.
